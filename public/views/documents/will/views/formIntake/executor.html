Personal Representative aka Executor

Your court appointed Personal Representative (in other states called the “executor”) makes sure your estate is settled upon your death. This ordinarily involves going through probate, a court-administered procedure for settling an estate as provided in your Will or under State law. Probate involves petitioning a court for letters of appointment, settling creditor claims, finding and distributing assets, and filing any necessary tax returns. Any competent adult may serve as your Personal Representative. Personal Representatives have a duty not only to the beneficiaries but also to the creditors of the estate.
